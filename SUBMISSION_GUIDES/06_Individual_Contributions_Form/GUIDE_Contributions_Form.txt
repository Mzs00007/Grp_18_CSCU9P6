================================================================================
FAIRNESS GUIDELINES
================================================================================

HONEST ASSESSMENT:
  • Be truthful about your contributions
  • Examiners verify against GitHub commits
  • 100% accuracy expected
  • Lies significantly undermine assessment

EQUAL WORK EXPECTATIONS:
  If you have 6 members:
    ✓ Each person should contribute 15-20% (roughly equal)
    ✓ Minor variations normal (±5% acceptable)
    ✗ One person with 50% suggests free-loading others
    ✗ Someone with 5% needs serious explanation

HANDLING UNEQUAL CONTRIBUTIONS:
  If someone did significantly more/less:
    • Explain why (illness, different roles, etc.)
    • Still maintain fairness in final grades
    • Examiners can adjust if evidence is clear
  
  NOT acceptable to claim dramatically unequal unless genuinely justified

COMMON SCENARIOS:

Scenario A: Person worked more (20-25%)
  Reason: Took technical lead, did core features
  Impact: Worth documenting but doesn't change their mark
  Note: All group members typically get same grade for group work

Scenario B: Person worked less (10-15%)
  Reason: Illness, personal circumstances
  Impact: Examiners may adjust if documented
  Note: Need explicit reason and timeline

Scenario C: Person did almost nothing (<5%)
  Red flag: Likely indicates free-loading
  Impact: Examiners will investigate, may result in failed assessment
  Note: This is taken very seriously - academic integrity issue

BEST PRACTICE:
  • All group members contribute reasonably equally (within 5-10%)
  • If someone falls behind, team helps them catch up
  • Document genuine difficulties with dates/evidence
